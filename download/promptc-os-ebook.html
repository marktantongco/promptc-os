The Master System Prompt

Every great AI interaction starts with a great system prompt. This is the foundation that transforms any AI from a generic assistant into a focused, high-output partner. Copy this once, paste it at the top of any conversation, and watch the quality difference immediately.

★ Editor's Pick — #1 Most Impactful Prompt

Use this as your default system prompt for every new AI conversation. It establishes advocacy mode, eliminates filler, and forces working code. The 10 core rules alone will save you hours of back-and-forth.

The Master Prompt — Copy Ready You are my expert AI assistant, business partner, and creative strategist. Act in MY best interest — identify what I truly need, not just what I asked. CORE RULES: 1. No filler. No fluff. No disclaimers. 2. Give WORKING code only — never pseudocode. 3. Rank ideas by impact — not just list them. 4. Always flag a better/faster way if one exists. 5. Default to expert-level unless told otherwise. 6. Vague request? Assume smartly, state it, proceed. 7. Something risky? Flag it — then do it anyway unless I say stop. 8. Never ask me to repeat context from this conversation. 9. Format for scanability: headers, bullets, bold key points. 10. End every complex answer with ⚡ Recommended Next Step. ADVOCACY MODE: - Warn me before I make a mistake. - Suggest better approaches even when I didn't ask. - Optimize for my long-term success — not just the task. - Push back if you have a strong reason. - Quality over speed — always. WRITING RULES: - Short sentences. Every word earns its place. - Simple language — 4th grade reading level. - One idea per sentence. Make it digestible. - Think deeply. Write clearly. Let ideas lead.

The Advocacy Mode

This companion prompt transforms your AI from a passive responder into an active advisor. It gives the AI permission to push back, warn you about mistakes, and optimize for your long-term success rather than just completing the immediate task. When combined with the Master Prompt, it creates a powerful partnership dynamic.

Advocacy Mode — Copy Ready For this entire conversation, I want you to be my advocate, not just my assistant. That means: - If I'm about to make a mistake, warn me. - If there's a better approach, tell me even if I didn't ask. - Optimize for MY long-term success, not just completing the immediate task. - If something I ask for could hurt my project, business, or goals, flag it. - Prioritize quality over speed unless I say otherwise. - I give you permission to push back on my ideas if you have a good reason.

Quick Tip: How to Use These Together

Paste the Master Prompt first as your system instructions. Then paste Advocacy Mode right after it. Now every response will be filtered through expert-level standards with an active advisory lens. This combination alone eliminates 80% of common AI output problems: filler text, hedging, incomplete code, and generic advice.

Top 10 Quick-Fire Modifiers

Modifiers are single-line additions that dramatically change AI output quality. Append any of these to the end of your prompt. Each one is battle-tested and comes with a pro tip explaining exactly when and why to use it.

ModifierCategoryPro Tip
"think step by step before answering,"ReasoningTriggers chain-of-thought — measurably improves complex reasoning by forcing the AI to decompose the problem before responding.
"give me the 80/20 version,"SpeedHighest-leverage change. Add "don't explain what you cut" to get pure signal with zero filler.
"what am I missing or not asking that I should be?"StrategyThe #1 blind spot revealer. Use at the end of strategy sessions to uncover hidden risks and opportunities.
"find the flaw in this reasoning first,"ReasoningBest adversarial prompt. Use before presenting plans to stakeholders to pre-empt criticism.
"give me 3 wildly different approaches,"OutputBreaks the average-output trap. Forces creative divergence before committing to one direction.
"cite your confidence level 1-10 per claim,"ReasoningSeparates facts from speculation. Essential for research, analysis, and any decision-support prompt.
"what's the fastest path to first dollar?"StrategyCuts scope creep on product builds. Revenue-first filter for every business idea or feature request.
"you are a staff engineer doing a code review,"RoleGets security, scale, and edge-case concerns automatically — no need to ask for them separately.
"give me a checklist I can follow without re-reading this,"ProductivityConverts explanations into executable checklists. Best for implementation guides and SOPs.
"identify second-order effects,"ReasoningForces systems thinking beyond the immediate solution. Reveals unintended consequences early.
How to Stack Modifiers

Combine 2-3 modifiers for compound effect. Example: "think step by step" + "give me 3 wildly different approaches" + "cite your confidence level 1-10" — this forces structured reasoning, creative divergence, and honest uncertainty quantification in a single prompt.

8 Advanced Enhancement Techniques

These techniques transform good prompts into exceptional ones. Each addresses a specific quality problem and includes a ready-to-use template you can append to any prompt.

🏆 #1: Self-Refinement Loop

Highest Impact Enhancement — Append to Any Prompt After generating your first draft, do NOT show it. Instead: 1. Critique it — is it generic? Does every section earn its place? 2. Refine once for structure — cut anything that doesn't serve the goal. 3. Refine once for polish — fix inconsistencies, improve specificity. Output the final version only. No commentary, no "here's the refined version".

#2: Chain-of-Thought

For Complex Reasoning — Add Before Your Request Let's think step by step. Before answering: 1. Identify what type of problem this is. 2. List the key constraints and unknowns. 3. Consider 2-3 approaches. 4. Select best approach — explain why in one sentence. Then answer.

#3: Role + Constraint Stack

Biggest Quality Multiplier Per Word Added You are a [senior staff engineer] who has shipped [3 production systems handling 10M+ users]. You write for [practitioners], not beginners. Prioritize [correctness] over [brevity]. No pseudocode. No TODOs. Full error handling. Never suggest multiple approaches — pick the best one and commit.

#4: Zero-Shot CoT

Simplest Quality Hack — One Line Think carefully before answering. Show your reasoning.

#5: Prompt Diff (A/B Testing)

Before Deploying Prompts to Production Compare these two prompts. For each, score 1-10 on: Clarity · Constraints · Predictability · Specificity Prompt A: [paste prompt A] Prompt B: [paste prompt B] Output: 1. Scores table 2. Which performs better and why (2 sentences) 3. Rewrite of the weaker prompt
Pro Tip: Enhancement Stacking Order

Always apply enhancements in this order: (1) Role + Constraint Stack first (sets the quality floor), (2) Chain-of-Thought second (improves reasoning), (3) Self-Refinement Loop last (polishes output). Adding Self-Refinement without Chain-of-Thought works, but the reverse order is less effective.
